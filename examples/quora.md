# Example: Quora Answer Humanization

## Input (AI-Generated — High Risk)

```
Starting a business requires careful planning and execution. It is important to note that
market research is a critical first step. Moreover, entrepreneurs must ensure they have
adequate funding before launching. Furthermore, building a strong team is essential for
long-term success. Consequently, aspiring business owners should approach this process
with diligence and patience.
```

## Output (Humanized — Low Risk)

```
I started my first business at 23 with basically no idea what I was doing. So take this
from someone who learned most of it the hard way.

The market research thing? Everyone says it, but almost nobody does it properly. I spent
four months building something nobody wanted because I asked the wrong people the wrong
questions. Talk to potential customers before you build anything — not friends and family,
actual strangers who have the problem you're solving.

Funding is trickier than it looks. You don't always need as much as you think, but you
need more runway than you expect. Most early-stage businesses fail not because the idea
was bad, but because they ran out of time before they figured it out.

Team matters more than the idea itself, honestly. A mediocre idea with great people will
beat a brilliant idea with the wrong people almost every time.

Patience is real. But so is urgency. The trick is knowing when to apply which.
```

## Key Changes Made

- Opened with personal experience hook
- Eliminated all AI transition phrases
- Added specific, concrete detail (4 months, talk to strangers, not friends)
- Used conversational asides ("honestly," "take this from someone who")
- Ended with a punchy insight rather than a formal summary
