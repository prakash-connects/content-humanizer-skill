# Example: Blog Post Paragraph Humanization

## Input (AI-generated, High Risk)

> "Artificial intelligence is revolutionizing the realm of content creation. It is worth noting that businesses are increasingly utilizing AI tools to streamline their content pipelines. Consequently, content marketers must adapt to these changes to remain competitive. In conclusion, embracing AI while maintaining authentic human voice is the key to success in the modern content landscape."

---

## Output (Humanized, Low Risk)

> "AI didn't sneak into content creation — it kicked the door in.
>
> Over the last two years, most marketing teams I know have quietly started using some form of it, whether they admit it publicly or not. The tools got good fast. Uncomfortably fast for some.
>
> Here's the tension nobody really talks about though: the content that performs best still reads like a person wrote it. Specific, opinionated, a little unpredictable. Not a summary of what everyone already knows.
>
> So the skill isn't learning to use AI. It's learning when to sound like yourself despite using it."

---

## Key Changes Made
- Removed "revolutionizing the realm", "It is worth noting", "utilizing", "Consequently", "In conclusion", "modern content landscape" (all Tier 1/2 bans)
- Opened with a punchy, specific image instead of a generic claim
- Added first-person social proof: "most marketing teams I know"
- Inserted a tension/counter-point structure (human reasoning pattern)
- Burstiness: 8 words → 22 words → 6 words → 18 words across paragraph
- Ended with an insight that feels arrived at, not announced
